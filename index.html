About
Me

Currently, I work at LSE IDEAS, the foreign policy think tank of the London School of Economics where I have spent a better part of my career honing my layout and typography skills as an in-house designer who occasionally helps new research centres with their brand rollouts.

I am an organised multitasker with a track record of working across a range of projects for academics and various stakeholders (from the financial, public and third sector) to produce various research reports and design materials, both digital and print, for our executive education programme, public lectures and off-the-record events.

I am quite confident about my skills in InDesign, Photoshop and Illustrator. This year, I started using Figma and Visual Studio Code for online courses on UX/UI Design and Interactive Data Visualisation (using HTML, CSS, SVG, and D3.js). I have also been tinkering with Adobe After Effects and the p5js javascript library for personal side projects.

I love making research look good and love it even more when the research team is happy and gets their message across. I am keen to work in an inclusive and progressive studio or startup environment, helping design projects that build a more sustainable world.
